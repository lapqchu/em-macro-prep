# EM Country Analysis — 90-Min Framework

**Purpose.** A structured workflow for going from "you've just been handed Country X" to "here's my view, here's the trade, here's the R:R" in 90 minutes. The framework is generic — it works for any EM country, any market regime.

**Companion files:**
- `EM_Monetary_Policy_Notes.md` — the 5 concepts (theory)
- `EM_Trade_Pitch_Framework.md` — turning a view into a tradeable expression
- `EM_Macro_Workbook.md` — exercises
- `EM_Macro_Reference.md` — quick reference

---

## The 90-Minute Time Budget (hard-coded)

| Block | Duration | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Orient** | 10 min | Read materials / pull data. Sketch country snapshot. Decide framework fit. |
| **2. Analyse** | 35 min | Work through 5 concepts in order. Note misfits. Form view. |
| **3. Trade idea** | 15 min | Look at curve. Identify mispricing. Define R:R. |
| **4. Build slides / note** | 25 min | 8-slide deck (or one-pager). Conclusion first. |
| **5. Buffer** | 5 min | Rehearse opening line + 30-sec elevator pitch. |

You don't choose the structure on the day. You execute it.

## The 8-Slide Note Structure (conclusion-first)

This is the same shape used by sell-side EM strategists at GS / JPM / Citi. **Slide 1 = your view.** Everything else is supporting evidence.

### Slide 1 — The View

- **Title**: e.g. *"Brazil: BCB Has Room to Cut 150bps More Than Market Prices — Receive 1y2y NDIRS"*
- **One-line view**: *"I expect BCB to deliver 150bps more easing than priced over the next 12 months because disinflation is broadening, output gap is widening negative, and FX is supportive."*
- **Trade**: *"Receive 1y2y BRL NDIRS at 11.00%, target 9.50%, stop 11.75%, R:R 2:1."*
- **3 supporting points** (one line each — expand into slides 3–5).

### Slide 2 — Cycle & Slack

- Real GDP growth, last 4 quarters vs trend
- Unemployment rate vs NAIRU (state source: IMF, OECD, local stats)
- Output gap estimate (state source / how computed)
- Capacity utilisation, business confidence, capex
- **One-sentence summary of cyclical position**

### Slide 3 — Inflation: Cyclical or Non-Cyclical?

- Headline CPI, core CPI, services inflation, goods inflation
- Decompose: how much FX-driven, food/energy, services?
- Wage growth, unit labour costs
- Inflation expectations (1y, 3y if surveyed)
- **Verdict: is inflation primarily slack-driven (Phillips), or supply-driven (FX/food/energy), or both?**

### Slide 4 — Taylor: Where Should Rates Be?

- State your r\* assumption explicitly
- Show the Taylor calc: **i\* ≈ r\* + π\* + 1.5(π − π\*) + 0.5·output_gap**
- Compare Taylor-implied rate to actual policy rate. State gap in bps.
- Compare Taylor-implied path vs market-implied path (WIRP / OIS curve / NDIRS)
- **Verdict: is the CB above, at, or below Taylor? Is the market pricing too hawkish/dovish a path?**

### Slide 5 — Transmission and FX Pass-Through

- Which channels are working? (interest rate / credit / asset price / FX / expectations)
- Dominant channel? (Almost always: FX in EM)
- Estimated FX pass-through (state source: BIS, IMF, local CB)
- Real ex-ante policy rate vs neutral. **Is policy actually restrictive?**

### Slide 6 — Where the Framework Misfits

This is the slide most analysts skip. Don't.

- What features of this country make the standard framework imperfect? (E.g. *"Egypt's CPI is heavily influenced by administered fuel/food prices, so domestic slack signals are muted. Pass-through is high and asymmetric. Fiscal dominance risk lingers."*)
- How does this change your view? Conviction higher or lower?

Cross-reference Booth's 4D risk lens from your `em-macro-trader` skill: high SDm / high Pr / low E countries often don't fit the textbook framework.

Senior PMs notice this slide.

### Slide 7 — The Curve & The Trade

- Show current curve. Label tenors: 3m, 1y, 2y, 5y, 10y
- Identify mispricing relative to your view
- State trade: instrument, tenor, entry, target, stop, sizing logic, R:R
- State invalidation: *"This trade is wrong if [specific data event]. I'd cut on [level]."*

### Slide 8 — Risks & Watch List

- 3 risks to the view (in order of severity)
- 2–3 key data prints in next 4 weeks that confirm or invalidate
- What you'd need to see to size up

## Quick Curve-Reading Cheat (for slide 7)

When you see the rates curve, ask:

1. **What is the market pricing for cuts/hikes over the next 12m?**
   - Use WIRP or compare today's 1y rate to today's policy rate.
   - 1y < policy rate by N bps → market pricing N bps of cuts over the year.
2. **Is the curve steeper or flatter than historical median?**
   - 2s10s, 5s10s, 1y2y are common pairs.
3. **Where is the terminal rate priced?**
   - Lowest point of the OIS/NDIRS forward curve.
4. **Real rate?**
   - Policy rate − inflation expectations.
5. **Cross-country relative value?**
   - Compare to neighbours / similar EM peers.

## Trade Expression Cheat-Sheet

| If your view is... | Trade |
|---|---|
| CB will cut more than priced in next 6m | Receive 6m1y / receive 3m6m |
| CB will cut more over 1–2 year horizon | Receive 1y2y or 2y3y (belly) |
| CB hold + curve too pessimistic at long-end | Receive 5y or 10y |
| CB cuts soon, long-end stays anchored | Bull-steepener (receive short, less long) or just receive short outright |
| Hike cycle priced too aggressive | Pay rates now, set tight stop |
| CB credibility under threat (or fiscal stress) | Bear-steepener (receive front, pay long) |
| FX-positive view + CB hawkish | Buy currency outright + pay short rates |
| Real rate too high → over-restrictive | Receive belly |

State in this format:

> *"Receive 1y2y BRL NDIRS, entry 11.00%, target 9.50% (−150bps), stop 11.75% (+75bps), R:R 2:1, sized at [N] DV01 to give [X] bps daily P&L vol. Invalidation: BCB hawkish hold at next meeting, or core inflation surprise above [Y]%."*

## Opening Line — Memorise the Structure

> *"My view on [Country] is [bullish / bearish / neutral] on rates, expressed via [trade]. The three reasons are [A], [B], and [C]. The single biggest risk is [D]."*

Conclusion-first. Trade-first. Risk-acknowledged. Done in 30 seconds.

---

## What to Do If You Don't Know

You'll be in unfamiliar territory regularly. Default lines:

- "I don't have that number in front of me, but my prior is X based on [Y]; I'd want to verify before sizing."
- "That's a good point I haven't fully thought through — my instinct is [Z], but I see the risk you're pointing to."
- "The framework doesn't extend cleanly there — let me think for a moment." [pause] "I'd say..."

**Never bluff a number.** Estimate openly and state the prior.
